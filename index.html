Announcement

It is with honour that we record the foundation of the United States Polo Patrons Association. This fellowship of patrons and players is inaugurated with Polo Patronium, a living token of support and tradition. Our purpose is simple: to safeguard the heritage of polo, encourage its growth, and open a new chapter in the life of the game.


Initiatives

Polo Patronium

A token and membership initiative uniting patrons, players, and clubs in a shared economy of sport.

The Polo Life

A platform dedicated to presenting the elegance and traditions of polo to new audiences in the digital age.

Charleston Polo

The renewal of Charleston, South Carolina’s polo tradition—our flagship chapter.

Cowboy Polo Circuit

A national endeavour to broaden the sport’s reach, nurture emerging talent, and encourage the next generation of American players.

Patronium Model

Stewardship-Based Rewards

Patronium is a utility token and seal of participation — not a security or speculative instrument. It rewards those who walk with us through principled alignment, not entitlement. Rewards may be distributed manually from active Polo Chapters when the mission allows.

Tribute Allocation Framework

Each active Chapter follows a 51/49 principle:
51% — Reinvested into horses, pasture, and operations
24.5% — Steward’s draw or groom-equivalent wage
24.5% — USPPA tribute pool for Patronium holders

In the first year, 49% flows to Founding Patrons instead — rewarding those who helped launch.

Claim, Don’t Stake

Rewards are earned, not promised. There is no staking, no yield farming, and no expectation of income. Snapshots may be taken to determine eligibility, and rewards are manually claimable based on alignment and stewardship.

Chapter Pipeline

USPPA Chapters begin with a Steward — a trained or approved individual — and a Polo Incubator funded by Founding Patrons. After 12 months of operations and public play, rewards stabilize, and the local operation matures into a Chapter.

Local stewards may be compensated with a groom-level wage or retain up to 24.5% of the net revenue, whichever is greater.
